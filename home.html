Kate Bush

Overview

Catherine Bush was born on July 30th, 1958. She debuted on the UK Single's Chart at age 19 with her single "Wuthering Heights". In doing so, she became the first female artist to top the chart with a self-written song. Bush had been writing songs since she was eleven years old. She was discovered by Pink Floyd guitarist David Gilmour and was signed to EMI at seventeen.

All of Kate Bush's 10 studio albums have reached the UK Top 10. Bush has collaborated with prominent musicians, among them Sir Elton John, Eric Clapton, Peter Gabriel, and Prince. She was made Commander of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire (CBE) in 2013 for services to music. In 2017, she was nominated for induction in the 2018 Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
